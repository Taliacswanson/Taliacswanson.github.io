ABOUT ME

My name is Talia C. Swanson, and I am currently a second-year Master of Public Health (MPH) student in the Epidemiology concentration. I completed my Bachelor of Science in Biology at the University of Georgia this past summer and am originally from Atlanta, Georgia.

RESEARCH INTERESTS & GOALS

My research interests focus on environmental exposures, particularly particulate matter (PM2.5), and how these exposures contribute to the development of chronic disease. I am also interested in social determinants of health, with an emphasis on how cultural choices and structural factors influence health outcomes.

In addition to my epidemiologic interests, in the ACCESS Lab under the Cellular Biology Department, I examine the undergraduate experience in STEM spaces, with a focus on how institutional structures and learning environments shape student engagement, retention, and success.

Long term I hope to work on environemntal exposures and how they contribute to respiratory and neurodegenerative disease while working in the federal sector.

DATA ANALYSIS EXPERIENCE

I have taken EPID 7010 and BIOS 7010/7020 that focused on using SAS to produce survival models, regression models, and ANOVA tests. I have also worked on an independent project assessing major depressive episodes in adolescents aged 12-17, examining the factors of culture and racial demographics for multivariate regression modeling using R. I hope to build on my skills in this class to become more confident in analyzing data as I hope to incorporate coding into my future career.
